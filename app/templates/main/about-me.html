My Story

My first goal after graduating college was to spend some time giving back to the community. I had recently read the statistic that nearly half of United States public schools had no computer science program of any kind and I knew right then that I needed to give back to field that had given me so much.

I was hired by one of the best public high school’s in the country with a single goal – to create and grow their computer science program. Over the next seven years, acting as the only computer science teacher in the district, I did just that. One course with 12 students quickly became four courses across six sections with over 100 students participating in the program. During this time I achieved a 100% passing rate on all of my student’s Advanced Placement exams and found time to create our competition team and also to teach AP statistics. With my initial goals largely accomplished, I set out to find the next step in my career.

Data science has always intrigued me. It feels as if someone took all the skills I had in programming, statistics and public speaking, combined them with the topics that interested me like machine learning, and packaged it all together into a career. I immediately knew the next step I wanted to take.

In between all this I always make sure I take the time to appreciate the small things in life. I have a number of hobbies from board games to traveling to adventure sports. I also enjoy personal coding projects and building computers. I love to discuss my favorite science fiction and fantasy properties and my next Comicon costume. I believe it is important to live a well rounded life and I want to experience as much as possible in my professional and personal life.


My Hard Skills

Since high school I have made computer science a core facet of my daily life. I am a proficient coder in Python (including Pandas, Numpy, Scikit-Learn, etc.), R (including Tidyverse) and MySQL. I have created a number of projects utilizing each of these technologies for graduate classes and as personal projects. In addition, I have substantial experience with Java, the web framework Flask and a number of front end web development technologies and organizational technologies such as Git. I have worked extensively in Windows, Linux and Mac environments.

During my 10+ years of post high school education I have taken classes in ‘Data Management’, ‘Business Analytics’, ‘Machine Learning & Big Data’, ‘Advanced Web Design’ and more. I bring with me a broad base of knowledge in a variety of technologies as well as a deep well of knowledge in my core areas of study.


My Soft Skills

In addition to the above hard skills, I also bring with me a number of core soft skills that will prove valuable. Owing to my years as a public high school teacher, I am a confident public speaker and talented at making presentations in front of crowds of all sizes. My presentations are engaging and fun, yet most importantly informative. I am adept at deciphering complex computer science topics and appropriately presenting them based on the audience.

I have a decade of experience working in a professional office environment and interacting with supervisors, bosses and parents. I am a consummate professional in all of my day-to-day interactions. This includes the delicate balance of accomplishing my required job tasks while ensuring that I have made the parent’s of my students as well as my coworkers happy.

Finally, more than anything else, I complete the tasks that need to be completed. Ask any of my references about my reputation and they will tell you that I am known as the person who gets things done. You will never have to chase me down to complete a project, submit paperwork, or contact a customer. I take it as a point of personal pride that I see something that needs to be done and I make sure that it gets done to a high level of quality. As an old professor once told me, ‘Early is on time and on time is late.’
