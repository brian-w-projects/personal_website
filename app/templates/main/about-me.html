My Story

My first goal after graduating college was to spend some time giving back to the community. I had recently read the statistic that nearly half of United States public schools had no computer science program of any kind and I knew right then that I needed to give back to field that had given me so much.

I was hired by one of the best public high schools in the country with a single goal – to create and grow their computer science program. Over the next seven years, acting as the only computer science teacher in the district, I did just that. One course with 12 students quickly became four courses across six sections with over 100 students participating in the program. During this time I achieved a 100% passing rate on all of my student’s Advanced Placement exams, found time to create our competition team and also to teach AP statistics. With my initial goals largely accomplished, I set out to find the next step in my career.

Data science has always intrigued me. It feels as if someone took all the skills I had in programming, statistics, mathematics and public speaking, combined them with the topics that interested me like machine learning, and packaged it all together into a career. I immediately knew the next step I wanted to take.

In between all this I always make sure to take the time to appreciate the small things in life. I have a number of hobbies from board gaming to traveling to adventure sports. I also enjoy personal coding projects and building computers. I love to discuss my favorite science fiction and fantasy properties and my next Comicon costume. I believe it is important to be well rounded and I want to experience as much as possible in my professional and personal life.


My Hard Skills

Since high school I have made computer science a core facet of my daily life. I am proficient in both Python and R (along with their data science libraries) and MySQL. I am also an Oracle Certified Professional Java programmer. From big data technologies to graph databases to Google Cloud Platform and beyond, if it has a role in data science odds are good that I have practical applied experience with it.

I also draw broadly from a background in software development which includes working with technologies like Git and Anaconda. My personal interest in all things programming has also resulted in exploration of Flask and other web development technologies. I often have a personal project or two that I’m working on just to explore something new.

During my 10 years of post high school education I have taken classes in ‘Data Management’, ‘Business Analytics’, ‘Machine Learning & Big Data’, ‘Advanced Web Design’, ‘Predictive Analytics’ and more. I bring with me a broad base of knowledge in a variety of technologies as well as a deep well of knowledge in my core areas of study. Most importantly, I bring with me a passion for my work and a desire succeed.


My Soft Skills

Owing to my years as a public high school teacher, I am a confident public speaker and talented at making presentations in front of crowds of all sizes. My presentations are engaging and enjoyable without losing sight of their purpose. I am adept at deciphering complex computer science topics and appropriately presenting them based on the audience.

I have a decade of experience working in an office environment and interacting professionally with supervisors, bosses and parental guardians. I am a consummate professional in all of my day-to-day interactions. I am comfortable taking on a lead role or sliding into a preexisting structure. There is no room for ego in a team environment.

My time as a teacher has also prepared me for a work environment with hard deadlines. While I have a strong intellectual curiosity that drives my work, I know that it is critical to focus on what is important and not just what is interesting. I model my work on the agile development cycle to ensure that I can present deliverables on time.
